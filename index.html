Graduates Staying In-Region

Gonzaga produces roughly 1,900 graduates annually. Spokane has ranked as the #1 destination city for GU graduates every measured year:ahead of Seattle, Portland, and every other metro. In 2022–23, 317 graduates named Spokane their first destination. With 11,855 degreed alumni in Spokane/North Idaho, Gonzaga is not just a pipeline to the national market:it is the primary engine keeping educated talent in the Inland Northwest.

Impact of Gonzaga Construction

Gonzaga has spent over a billion dollars in the last 25 years building, expanding, and renovating campus buildings. The university has consistently been the top contributor to the construction market in Spokane and the entire Inland Northwest. From the School of Law in 2000 to Mantua Hall in 2025, every major project injected capital directly into Spokane's construction workforce, supplier network, and subcontractor ecosystem — sustaining an average of 251 construction jobs annually over the period.

Gonzaga's Civic Role in Spokane

Gonzaga graduates don't only enter Spokane's private economy:they staff its civic and nonprofit backbone. Social services, legal aid, public health, education, and community advocacy organizations across the region rely on a steady stream of GU-trained professionals. As a Jesuit institution, Gonzaga's mission explicitly connects academic education to community service, and the employment data reflects that: graduates appear across every civic sector in Spokane County.

Access:Pell-Eligible & First-Generation Enrollment

Pell Grant eligibility is the federal benchmark for low-income student access. Gonzaga's Pell-eligible enrollment peaked at 897 in 2014, dipped through the late 2000s, and has rebounded sharply:reaching nearly 1,000 students in 2025. First-generation enrollment has likewise grown, reaching 17% of the student body in 2024–25.

Outcomes:Upward Mobility

Among students enrolled at Gonzaga in the early 2000s, 59% of lower-income students reached the top 40% of earners in adulthood:a meaningful mobility rate for a private institution. For comparison, Whitworth College enrolls a higher share of students from the bottom 40% of incomes but achieves a lower mobility score, illustrating that access alone is not enough:outcomes matter.

Source: New York Times / Opportunity Insights, "Some Colleges Have More Students From the Top 1 Percent Than the Bottom 60" (2017), based roughly on 2002–4 graduating classes.

Local Pathways:Gonzaga as a Spokane Institution

Gonzaga draws disproportionately from its own backyard:and that share is growing. In 2025–26, 36% of students came from Spokane County, more than double the 17.6% share in 2014–15. Among Washington community college transfers, Spokane schools dominate: Spokane Falls Community College alone accounted for 42.4% of all WA CC transfers to Gonzaga from 2011–17.

In 2008 and 2020, when Spokane's private economy contracted, Gonzaga kept hiring, building, and placing graduates. As a mission-driven institution, it doesn't cut payroll on a quarterly cycle — making it one of the county's most stable economic anchors through both downturns.
